What happens to the archaic human in a digital, AI-driven, hyper-networked world? I approach this question using the ideas of the French-American anthropologist and scholar René Girard. Below are some rather speculative and explorative talks and discussions with anthropologists, philosophers, artists and futurologists on this subject. Caveat emptor!

Picture: "The fight between carnival and lent", Pieter Bruegel the Elder, 1559 (Wikipedia). The perennial fight between lent (representing prohibition and profane time) and carnival (representing transgression and sacred time) is the perfect anthropological metaphor for our times.
