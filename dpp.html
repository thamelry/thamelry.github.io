Deep probabilistic programming (DPP) combines the scope of deep learning with the principled uncertainty modeling of Bayesian statistics. The DPP group spans the departments of biology and computer science at the University of Copenhagen and focuses on both theory and applications of DPP. Main focus areas are Bayesian deep learning, high performance computing for machine learning, and DPP applications in life sciences (protein structure and evolution, RNA biology). We mainly use (and contribute to) the DPP languages Pyro and Numpyro. Recent highlights include:

  • A deep probabilistic model of protein evolution (ICLR, 2022).
  • Bayesian inference of deep models using Stein Mixtures (ICLR, 2025).
  • A generalized Bayesian interpretation of AlphaFold (AISTATS, 2026).
